Methodology builder

Step 1 · Study framing
Methodology decisions make more sense when the study purpose is visible. Keep this concise but real.
Step 2 · Overall methodology approach
Choose the family of methodology that actually matches the evidence you need and the claims you want to make.
Step 3 · Research design
A design is the architecture inside the approach. Pick something recognizable enough that a committee would know what you mean.
Step 4 · Participants, sources, and sampling
Who or what will provide the evidence, and how will you justify the sample instead of just collecting what is easiest?
The options shift based on the overall approach.
Step 5 · Data collection and instrumentation
Methodology gets real here. Choose what evidence will actually be gathered and how it will be gathered.
Step 6 · Quality standards and researcher stance
Strong methods explain how quality will be established, not just what data will be collected.
Step 7 · Ethics and feasibility
A beautiful methodology that cannot get approved, accessed, or completed is not a viable methodology.
Step 8 · Data analysis plan
Say exactly how the evidence will be analyzed. This is where vague methodology sections fall apart.
